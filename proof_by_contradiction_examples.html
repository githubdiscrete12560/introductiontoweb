Proof by Contradiction

Three Classic Examples of Mathematical Reasoning

What is Proof by Contradiction?

Proof by contradiction (also called reductio ad absurdum) is a powerful mathematical technique where we:

  • Assume the opposite of what we want to prove
  • Use logical reasoning to derive consequences
  • Arrive at a contradiction (something impossible)
  • Conclude that our assumption must be false
  • Therefore, the original statement must be true

General Strategy

  1. Assume ¬¨P (the negation of what you want to prove)
  2. Apply logical reasoning and known facts
  3. Derive a contradiction (something both true and false)
  4. Conclude ¬¨P is false, so P must be true
