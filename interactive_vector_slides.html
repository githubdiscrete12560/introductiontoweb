Vector Dot Product

The Foundation of Spatial Relationships

a · b = a₁b₁ + a₂b₂ + a₃b₃ = |a| |b| cos(θ)

🔢 Algebraic Definition

For vectors a = (a₁, a₂, a₃) and b = (b₁, b₂, b₃):

a · b = a₁ × b₁ + a₂ × b₂ + a₃ × b₃

📐 Geometric Definition

The dot product measures how much two vectors point in the same direction:

a · b = |a| × |b| × cos(angle)

Dot Product Interpretation

Understanding the Results

Dot Product Value Angle Range Relationship Meaning
1.0 0° Perfect alignment Vectors point in exactly the same direction
0.5 to 1.0 0° to 60° Similar direction Vectors point mostly in the same direction
0.0 90° Perpendicular Vectors are orthogonal (sideways)
-0.5 to 0.0 90° to 120° Mostly opposite Vectors point somewhat in opposite directions
-1.0 180° Perfect opposition Vectors point in exactly opposite directions

💡 Real-World Example

Imagine two people walking:

  • Dot = 1: Walking in the same direction
  • Dot = 0: Walking perpendicular paths
  • Dot = -1: Walking toward each other

Vector Cross Product

Creating Perpendicular Vectors

a × b = (a₂b₃ - a₃b₂, a₃b₁ - a₁b₃, a₁b₂ - a₂b₁)

🔄 What is Cross Product?

The cross product takes two vectors and returns a new vector perpendicular to both.

  • Input: Two 3D vectors
  • Output: One 3D vector
  • Direction: Right-hand rule
  • Magnitude: Area of parallelogram

✋ Right-Hand Rule

Point fingers along first vector, curl toward second vector, thumb points in cross product direction.

Special Cases:
• Parallel vectors: a × b = 0
• Unit vectors: |a × b| = |a| |b| sin(θ)
