Rational vs Irrational Numbers

Understanding the fundamental difference between rational and irrational numbers

📚 Definitions

✅ Rational Numbers

Definition: Numbers that can be expressed as a fraction a/b where a and b are integers and b ≠ 0.


Decimal Pattern: Either terminates (ends) or repeats infinitely.


Examples: 1/2, 0.75, 5, 0.333..., -7

❌ Irrational Numbers

Definition: Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of integers.


Decimal Pattern: Never-ending, non-repeating decimal expansion.


Examples: π, √2, √3, e, φ (golden ratio)

🔑 Key Points to Remember

  • All integers are rational because they can be written as n/1
  • Terminating decimals are rational (like 0.75 = 3/4)
  • Repeating decimals are rational (like 0.333... = 1/3)
  • Square roots of non-perfect squares are irrational (like √2, √3, √5)
  • Famous constants like π and e are irrational
  • The sum of rational and irrational is always irrational

🧮 How to Identify:

✅ It's Rational if:

  • You can write it as a/b (fraction)
  • Decimal ends (like 0.75)
  • Decimal repeats (like 0.333...)
  • It's a whole number

❌ It's Irrational if:

  • Cannot be written as a fraction
  • Decimal never ends and never repeats
  • It's √(non-perfect square)
  • It's π, e, or similar constants
